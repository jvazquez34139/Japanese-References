Introduction

私は シラス です There's a few things I want to explain about this page. Starting off with the main purpose. I want to help give some guidence to anyone interested in learning how to speak and read Japanese as a self taught learner. I went through several free and paid sources and services to know which are appropriate during any level of the learners journey. A few of these would have saved me lot's of time had i known about them from the beginning. This is the main reason for this article. I want to save new self-taught students a lot of time in their endeavors by pointing them in a somewhat organized approach for tackling the different obstacles faced when learning Japanese from zero experience.


I started from 0 knowledge since I didnt have the option to learn this language in school but i was very interested in several things about it. About Japan as a country in general. The anime, food, and their culture all together makes learning japanese so interesting. Knowing the language could even land me a job over seas one day.


Now to explain how this page works.


- I have to say that I am not sponsored or paid to advertise any of these resources. This is all entirely just my honest opinion of these resources. Nor am I making any claim that anyone can self teach themselves using any of the methods mentioned in this page. Nor claiming that the resources i mentioned are the best suited for everybodies specific learning styles. It will all depend on how much time is put into the learning, focus, and determination of the reader.


- I will mention resources such as physical books, android apps, webpages, and youtube channels, and i will talk about what comes included in free and paid packages. Screenshots will also be provided.


- You dont need to read through the whole article. If you Have a prefered platform or method of learning you can skip to the section using the navigation bar. In fact most of the resources will have just about anything you need to get started and to learn the language if you put enough time and effort.


- I will give tips i read in many resources, mentioned here or not, that i've read since writing this article. I will also give my own opinion of where to start and where a student can go next to progress as smooth as possible when learning Japanese.


- I will also give my own thoughts of what certain apps or resources could have had to make learning more efficient for the user/reader.
